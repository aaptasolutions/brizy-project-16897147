Social Media Content

The evolution of Internet has led to some revolutionary communication platforms like social media. iTransmaster has understood the marketing potential of this and the essence of it being real-time, fresh and vibrant. With proper planning we generate content that connects the reader with the organizations standpoint on various relevant subjects. The staggering count of social media statistics speak for themselves, and articulate the importance of having a near impeccable social media content in place. Powered with weapons like tweets, blog-posts, blog-comments and carefully crafted profile pages on Facebook, LinkedIn, Google+ , this gives an organization the second level of outreach to the target audience.

With regards to technical, learning-based and consultative content; wiki-blogs, forums, and discussion pads provide an esplanade to members to build an open-ended interaction and information sharing platform. Such facets of social media are always an added asset to any organizations client/customer support group. We at iTransmaster help you put together these blocks of social media, to design your own customized communication.

Research Based Content

We at iTransmaster, research and write customised reports, white-papers and studies for clients. These publications are researched, written, and designed to provide your audiences with substantive information. Regardless of domain, the objective is to write clearly and concisely about your research topic so that the reader can easily understand the purpose and results of your research.

Our strategies to drill content on the subject are aligned to the clients focus and approach on the final expected output, thus researched content on the same topic for two different clients is never the same. The source considered for research are discussed with clients and if required specific approvals are sought. The information research group at iTransmaster has expertise in identifying various probable sources to pick specific content based on the subject, type and depth of the information required.
